Past Projects

QA Equipment Design

The objective of this project was to redesign new quality assurance equipment for nuclear fuel. To do this, I analyzed partial drawings of previously designed equipment and created new 3D CAD models. I also had to complete data analysis on the fuel in instances where there was no history of acceptable values for the parameters the equipment was designed to measure.

Once the models were complete, I created engineering drawings and manufacturing instructions so that the equipment could be implemented within the facility.

Synthetic Bone Graft Development

This project consisted of the design and development of a 3D-printed synthetic bone scaffold through two iterations of prototyping. The design needed to fit the dimensional constraints of a young athletes upper-tibia and maximize porosity to allow for natural skeletal growth, all while remaining structurally capable to support their physical needs.

My role in this project was to assist the other members of my group in generating potential designs, focusing on the analysis of the biomechanics that the problem posed and the FEA that followed. My team and I then completed physical testing of our first prototype during which I adjusted my FEA method and applied it to our second design which reached the goals our team had set at the beginning of the project.

Canadian Hydrogen Generation Potential

This project was solely theoretical and required my team to research innovative methods of hydrogen generation that would be applicable within Canada. The proposed method of hydrogen needed to be at least net-zero in terms of carbon emissions.

I lead my groups research into methods of energy production that would power the hydrogen generation process. The optimal method of power generation was found to be hydrogen generation, which would power a PEM electrolysis system. I then determined a suitable location for our design, comparing the potential turbine types, and completing a cost analysis. The result was a proposal for a plant that would supply sufficient fuel for the back-up generators of a hospital in rural Quebec, accompanied by a breakdown of the required hydroelectric plant, and life-time cost analysis.


Material Handling Equipment Safety Verification

For this project, I was tasked with ensuring the structural safety of all the manual material handling equipment within a manufacturing plant. This included CAD models and drawings of undocumented equipment, FEA analysis, risk assessment for each individual piece of equipment, and implemting appropriate safety measures.

To complete this project, I needed to ensure every piece of equipment was suited for its use and had an inspection plan in place, appropriate to the risk a potential incident with the equipment posed. The equipment then needed to be documented within the plant's data storage software. This would allow any incidents or concerns regarding the equipment's functionality to be recorded and trigger appropriate action-requests from plant supervisors.
